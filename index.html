Gindiri Theological Seminary (G.T.S.)

Owned by Church of Christ in Nations ‘COCIN’ (hereafter referred to as the Church). The Seminary strives to maintain a balanced spirituality, including a high sense of Christian maturity and rigorous academic life. Theology without ethics is like being a Christian without Christ and the Bible.

About G.T.S.

VISION STATEMENT

GTS envisions a contextual seminary that is a centre of excellence in theological education, with adequate infrastructural facilities, faciliated by qualified and well-motivated staff who are training students for wholistic ministry.

MISSION STATEMENT

GTS exists to glorify God through her services to Him and humanity by providing excellent theological education for wholistic ministry.

Brief History

The Bible school which has today metamorphosed into a Gindiri Theological Seminary was the first school to be established on the Gindiri Compound even though within the womb of this was the simultaneous establishment of the Gindiri Teachers’ College. The two schools were therefroe established as two-in-one arrangement when 18 students were admitted into the school as the first fruits of the centre. With great excitement and determination, the initial instruction which was given in the school hall of the Bible was in Hausa.

The curriculum of the school was diversified into many strands which included literacy, a theological academic content and modern Agricultural training. This wholistic approach was predicated on the fact that the students, who were being trained as evangelist, were going to be church workers on regular salary by the scheme of service of the early life of the church. This additional training was therefore put in place to prepare them on how to earn a livelihood through improved and accelerated agricultural production, at the same time as they served as evangelists

Although not all the first 15 trainees actually became ordained pastors, it was from amongst them that came the first generation of COCIN Pastors. The training of evangelists for the growing African church continued in Gindiri for about 3 decades before the school was upgraded into a Pastors’ College in 1963. With this development, which was the first turning point in the history of the institution, many of the former students returned to be trained as pastors.

A major feature of education offered in the Bible school and Pastors’ College Gindiri was its basic Hausa medium of expression. Its inadequacy began to clearly manifest with the rapid expansion of western education especially in the post independence period. The needs of the Gindiri Schools were strong enough to constrain the SUM to reconsider this approach with many students attending the weekly Sunday services, instruction in English became imperative. Time had come to train English speaking pastors to meet the needs of English congregations in the church.

The mandate of the College continued to revolve around three major concerns. Firstly, the College training environment was carefully calculated to help the students reconcile their profession with the Christian faith. This was the most crucial concern of the College which has to do with the life changing encounter the believer has in Christ Jesus. The College, at the second level, while believing that it was a tool in God’s hands to tranform the lives of the students, also made a deep commitment to mould them as future leaders of the church. It was believed that they would one day take over from the European missionaries. For this reason, the training given them was done with extra tenacity to ensure the church was not left in the hands of a poor leadership clergy but in one that was strong and reliable.

The third concern of the school was the training of religious instructors for the schools of both the Government and the Missionary organizations. This was considered a vital link in the efforts of the missionaries. The school was therefore, actually a discipleship arrnagement intended to raise a crop of religious instructors that would pass on the message of the Gospel of Jesus christ through the school system.

The Bible School which was re-christened College of Theology began with a four-year Diploma program in Theology and Christian Education. Its affiliation to Ahmadu Bello University Zaria was discontinued in 1997 after which it was affiliated to the University of Jos. This paved the way for the College to make yet anotehr significant turning point when in the year 2000, a Bachelor of Divinity degree program was introduced. A new degree program has just been introduced- B.A in Christian Religious Studies.

Since inception in 1934, the College has experienced quantum structural development from the era of round huts to modern solid structures especially in the last decade. With this came corresponding increases in students’ population. Unlike the other Gindiri schools, the College of Theology has depended solely on the church for its captial and recurrent expenditure. Thus, the cost of buidling classrooms, residential quarters and other facilities required by the school and the emoluments of staff and other bills are borne by the church. It is in this context and national and global socio-economic environment that the last one decade of the institution became very significant. The authorities of the College under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Soja Bewarang came up with a wonderful plan for its development. Under the direction of the Holy Spirit, they developed an approach which they called the “Faith Project”. The plan was for the construction of a giant hostel village for the students of the college with the labour of the College community acting as a mustard seed. The church individuals and oversea donor groups and individuals were targeted at second level, for the realization of the dream-hence the use of the term “Faith Project” to capture this great effort to push forward the frontiers of the College. To the glory of God, the plan worked and the project was completed successfully in the year 2000. Apart from the Faith Project, the College has under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Obed G.K. Dashan, continued to transform the structural landscape of the institution. Using almost the same tools, especially in the land mark construction. In the same vein, the College continues with its vision as identified in the 2009-2014 Strategic Plan. Since inception in 1934, the College has been headed by the following as Principals or Provosts

On going Projects

Administrative Block!

A modern one-story building which is the administration block was erected during the period under review. The building is made-up of twenty three (23( offices, a board room, staff room, and a computer poll

Post-graduate school Structure!

The Seminary is now building a two-story Post-graduate school. We have completed building work on the ground and the first; work on the second floor is now on going. This building when completed will accommodate principal officers of the PG school, lecture halls, and a modern e-library

Outreach!

The Seminary recently embarked on an Outreach in conjunction with COCIN RCC Bwai. This Outreach was successful and so many people rededicated their lives to Christ while others received salvation in Christ.

Purchased of books!

The Seminary purchased books covering various fields of study for the library amounting to almost Two Million Naira (N2, 000, 000.00).

The Seminary has received the approval of the commencement of Post Graduate Programme and the first set of candidate have been duly examined admissions are in progress
